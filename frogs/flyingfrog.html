Wallace's Flying Frogs

These frogs are found in western Indonesia, and are named for Alfred R. Wallace, the biologist who collected the first known specimen of this species. Interestingly, male flying frogs are smaller than females. Flying frogs have long limbs with thin skin attached, as well as webbing between their fingers and toes. This skin is what allows them to glide between the branches of the trees where they reside.

Their diet is made up mostly of insects, although they have been known to eat toads and small birds as well.
