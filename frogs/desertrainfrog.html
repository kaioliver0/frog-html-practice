Desert Rain Frogs

This species of frog is native to Namibia and South Africa and is known for it's short head and round body. It's natural habitat is in a narrow strip of sandy shores. As tourism and mining continue to harm their ecosystem, they are sadly becoming endangered.

Desert rain frogs are nocturnal, and spend most of the daytime in a burrow surrounded by moist sand. Although they hatch eggs, unlike other frogs they do not have a tadpole stage. They are also well known for the high pitched squeaking sounds they make when threatened, which has become the central focus of several viral videos.
